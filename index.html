Trigger is the smaller of 20% of BSLs or 200 BSLs from a single project, CAIs included. Source: Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance, p.4; FAQ V21, 9.7, p.65

How this tool routes, with sources:

  • Reason Codes 1 to 3 are mandatory during the period of performance and carry direct authority, reported via the SAR. Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance, p.4
  • Reason Codes 4 to 6 carry direct authority only if the drop stays at or below the smaller of 20% / 200, CAIs included; above that a New No BEAD Locations request is required. Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance, p.4; FAQ V21, 9.7, p.65
  • Reason Code 7 requires NTIA approval before moving the BSLs to the No BEAD list, regardless of count. Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance, p.4
  • Any change in technology type, even partial, requires NTIA approval regardless of count. Scope Change & Budget Modification Primer, Table 1
  • The baseline rolls: each approved reduction becomes the new approved project area, so drops are not cumulative against the original Final Proposal. FAQ V21, 9.7, p.65
  • A scope change request runs through NGP and requires resubmission of all Final Proposal CSVs, budget documentation, and narrative responses; wait for the NOA amendment or administrative letter before implementing. Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance, pp.6, 17; Primer, Change Request Submission Process
